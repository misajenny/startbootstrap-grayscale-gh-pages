About Me

I primarily work as a community organizer. Most of my work has been concentrated in combatting gender-based violence in the South Asian and Indo-Caribbean community as well as fighting for progressive immigration, racial justice, and educational justice policies. My interest also lies in using research, technology, photography, and cinematography to aid social movement organizations in raising awareness and being able to more effectively strategize and campaign.

My Films

Cinematography is a powerful artistic and storytelling tool that can beautifully and subtly expose and critique the injustices that people become numb and indifferent about in our world. It captures viewers' attention in a unique way that is unlike other forms of media and methods, and is quite effective in raising consciousness and mobilizing people around global issues. In the past few years, I have been exploring how I can use this tool to involve people and compel them to join social movements for justice and peace globally.

Research

I have been able to build skills in data collection both for social science and scientific purposes, from conducting surveys to extracting DNA and morphological information, and data analysis. However, for me, research is only the first step for making social change, it is just as, if not more, important to then use the research for policy or other cultural/methodical recommendations in order to create a better, more just, and sustainable world.

Title: The Effect of School Policing on New York City Public High Schools

Institution: Townsend Harris High School

Years: 2015-2016

Description: With the increase of criminalization and mass incarceration of working-class and people of color in the United States in recent years, this research investigates what many scholars know as the school-to-prison pipeline in New York City public high schools using historical and survey data.

Title: Low Morphological Diversity in Sturnus vulgaris Across the United States

Institution: American Museum of Natural History

Years: 2015-2016

Description: We have seen, in recent scientific history, an alarming growing number of species becoming extinct, due to in large part of low genetic diversity. This study investigates the success of starlings, a species of bird that has done surprisingly well in many parts of the world it has invaded, despite its low genetic diversity.
